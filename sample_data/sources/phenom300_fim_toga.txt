FIM-22-10-00 — TOGA Switch Fault Isolation
Embraer Phenom 300 — Fault Isolation Manual, Rev 14
Aircraft: Phenom 300
ATA 22 Auto Flight

Symptom: TOGA lever button not working — autoflight does not transition
to TOGA mode when the side-stick TOGA pushbutton is pressed at any
phase of flight.

Likely causes (ordered by frequency in fleet history):
1. Failed TOGA pushbutton micro-switch on the captain or first officer
   side-stick. The micro-switch is a discrete input to the FGC
   (Flight Guidance Computer) via the side-stick electronics module.
2. Open or chafed wire between the TOGA pushbutton and the FGC discrete
   input pin on connector P22-A12 — see WDM 22-10-04 for the wiring run.
3. Failed FGC discrete input channel (less common; requires FGC swap).
4. Software config drift after avionics load — verify with the post-load
   functional check listed in AMM 22-10-15.

Step-by-step isolation:
- Step 1: With aircraft on the ground, electrical power applied,
  perform the cockpit BIT and confirm the FGC reports no faults on
  the discrete input page.
- Step 2: Press the TOGA pushbutton on each side stick in turn while
  observing the FGC discrete input page. A working pushbutton causes
  the TOGA discrete to transition LOW->HIGH momentarily.
- Step 3: If the discrete does not transition on a given side, swap
  the TOGA pushbutton with the spare per AMM 22-10-22 and retest.
- Step 4: If the discrete still does not transition, perform the
  continuity check from connector P22-A12 pin 14 to the side-stick
  TOGA pushbutton return per WDM 22-10-04.
- Step 5: If continuity is good, replace the FGC per AMM 22-10-30 and
  perform the post-installation functional test.

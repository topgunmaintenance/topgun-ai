# Topgun AI — Product Brief

**Topgun AI is AI maintenance intelligence for aviation teams.**

## The problem

Aviation maintenance organizations drown in paperwork. An AMM is thousands
of pages. Work orders, logbooks, and troubleshooting records are scattered
across PDFs, scans, and printouts. Mechanics burn hours hunting for a
procedure, a torque value, a part number, or a history of a recurring
fault — and every wrong answer costs money, delays a flight, or creates a
safety risk.

## The product

Topgun AI ingests manuals, records, parts catalogs, bulletins, and scanned
sign-offs. It structures them into a queryable knowledge layer. Mechanics,
planners, and operators ask natural-language questions and get fast,
source-cited answers that look like a mission-critical intelligence panel
— not a chat bubble.

## The users

- **Line mechanics** — "What are the steps to replace the left starter-
  generator on the Citation XLS?"
- **Troubleshooters** — "What are the likely causes of hydraulic pressure
  fluctuation on N-tail-X?"
- **Planners** — "What inspections are due in the next 60 days across the
  fleet?" and "Which parts should we stock based on last year's usage?"
- **Inspectors** — "Show the sign-off history for this component."
- **Directors of Maintenance** — "Which aircraft have recurring bleed-air
  over-temp write-ups?"

## What Topgun AI guarantees

1. **Source citations on every answer.** If no source supports it, the
   answer says so.
2. **No invented part numbers, torque values, or procedural steps.**
3. **Explicit confidence.** Low-confidence answers are clearly marked.
4. **Full audit trail.** Every query, ingestion, and edit is logged.
5. **Mobile-first** for the hangar. Works on a phone on the ramp.

## Why now

- LLMs finally good enough to synthesize structured, cited answers.
- Affordable vector search (pgvector) and high-quality embeddings.
- Aviation maintenance organizations are ready for AI that respects their
  audit and safety culture — but existing chatbot tools do not.

## Success metric

Minutes saved per mechanic per shift on information retrieval, measured
via a baseline time-motion study before rollout and again after 30 days.
Target: ≥ 20 minutes saved per mechanic per shift in the first month.
